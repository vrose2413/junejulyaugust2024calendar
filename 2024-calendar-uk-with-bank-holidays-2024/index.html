What are Bank Holidays?

Bank holidays are public holidays in the UK when banks, schools, and most businesses close. These holidays are determined by the government and are usually on a Monday, giving people a long weekend. There are eight bank holidays in England and Wales, nine in Scotland, and ten in Northern Ireland.

What are the Bank Holidays in 2024?

The bank holidays in England and Wales for 2024 are:

  • New Year's Day - 1st January
  • Good Friday - 19th April
  • Easter Monday - 22nd April
  • Early May Bank Holiday - 6th May
  • Spring Bank Holiday - 27th May
  • Summer Bank Holiday - 26th August
  • Christmas Day - 25th December
  • Boxing Day - 26th December

The bank holidays in Scotland for 2024 are the same as in England and Wales, but there's an additional holiday on 2nd January, which is a replacement holiday for New Year's Day falling on a Sunday.

The bank holidays in Northern Ireland for 2024 are:

  • New Year's Day - 1st January
