What is the RMEF?

The Rocky Mountain Elk Foundation is a non-profit organization dedicated to the conservation of elk and their habitat. Founded in 1984, the organization has worked tirelessly to protect elk populations across North America. The RMEF calendar is one of the organization's fundraising initiatives.

What Does the Calendar Feature?

The RMEF calendar for 2024 features twelve stunning images of elk in their natural habitat. Each month features a different image, and the calendar also includes important dates such as hunting seasons, conservation events, and holidays.

The RMEF calendar not only features stunning images of elk but also includes important dates related to conservation and hunting. It is a great way to stay informed about the issues facing elk populations and to support their conservation.

Why Should You Get the RMEF Calendar?

The RMEF calendar is more than just a way to keep track of dates. By purchasing the calendar, you are supporting the conservation of elk and their habitat. The proceeds from the calendar go towards funding RMEF's conservation projects, which include habitat restoration, research, and advocacy.

How Does the RMEF Help Elk Populations?

The RMEF has been instrumental in protecting elk populations across North America. The organization funds research into elk populations and habitat, advocates for policies that benefit elk, and works to restore degraded habitats. The RMEF has also been involved in reintroducing elk to areas where they were once extinct.

The RMEF has funded a variety of conservation projects, including habitat restoration in the Appalachian Mountains, research into the impact of climate change on elk populations, and the reintroduction of elk to the Great Smoky Mountains National Park.
