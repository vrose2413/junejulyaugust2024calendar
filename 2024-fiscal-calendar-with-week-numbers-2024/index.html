What is a Fiscal Calendar?

A fiscal calendar is a system used by businesses and organizations to track financial periods. It is different from a traditional calendar in that it begins and ends on dates that are not necessarily the same as the standard calendar year. This allows businesses to align their financial reports with their operations, which may not follow the traditional calendar year.

Why are Week Numbers Important?

Week numbers are an important part of the fiscal calendar system. They allow businesses to track financial periods more precisely and make it easier to compare financial data across time periods. For example, if a business wants to compare sales figures for the first six weeks of 2024 with the first six weeks of 2023, they can easily do so using week numbers.
