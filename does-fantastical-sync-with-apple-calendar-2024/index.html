Does Fantastical Sync With Apple Calendar 2024?

Written by Mable Stanley Apr 28, 2023 · 3 min read

If you’re an Apple user, then you’re probably familiar with the built-in calendar app called Apple Calendar. It’s a simple and effective way to keep track of your schedule. However, some users prefer to use third-party apps to manage their calendars, and one such app is Fantastical.

The Basics of Fantastical and Apple Calendar

If you’re an Apple user, then you’re probably familiar with the built-in calendar app called Apple Calendar. It’s a simple and effective way to keep track of your schedule. However, some users prefer to use third-party apps to manage their calendars, and one such app is Fantastical.

Fantastical is a popular calendar app that offers more features and customization options than the Apple Calendar. It’s available for both iOS and macOS, and it’s known for its natural language input, which makes adding events quick and easy.

So, the question is, does Fantastical sync with Apple Calendar 2024?

The Short Answer

The short answer is yes. Fantastical syncs with Apple Calendar seamlessly, so you don’t have to worry about missing any events or appointments. In fact, Fantastical uses the same calendar database as Apple Calendar, so any changes you make in one app will automatically be reflected in the other.

But, how does it work?

The Long Answer

When you first install Fantastical, it will ask for permission to access your Apple Calendar data. Once you grant permission, Fantastical will start syncing with your Apple Calendar, and any events or appointments you have in Apple Calendar will appear in Fantastical.

If you add or edit an event in Fantastical, it will be synced back to Apple Calendar, and vice versa. This means that you can use either app to manage your calendar, and all the changes will be reflected in both apps.

Additionally, Fantastical offers some features that are not available in Apple Calendar, such as the ability to create calendar sets, which allow you to group related calendars together. You can also use Fantastical to create and manage tasks and reminders, which can be synced with Apple Reminders.

What are the benefits of using Fantastical?

The Benefits of Using Fantastical

There are several benefits to using Fantastical instead of Apple Calendar. First, Fantastical offers more customization options, such as the ability to change the color and font of your calendar. You can also choose whether to display events in a list or a calendar view, and you can customize the date and time formats.

Second, Fantastical’s natural language input is a game-changer. Instead of having to navigate through menus and fill out forms, you can simply type or dictate your event in plain English, and Fantastical will understand and add it to your calendar.

Finally, Fantastical is available on both iOS and macOS, so you can use it on all your Apple devices. This makes it easy to stay organized and on top of your schedule, no matter where you are.

Is there a downside to using Fantastical?

The Downside of Using Fantastical

One potential downside of using Fantastical is that it’s not free. While Apple Calendar is included with your Apple devices, you’ll need to purchase Fantastical separately. However, many users find that the additional features and functionality are worth the cost.

Another downside is that Fantastical may not be as intuitive as Apple Calendar for some users. While the natural language input is a great feature, it may take some time to get used to, especially if you’re used to the traditional form-based input.

Conclusion

In conclusion, if you’re looking for a more powerful and customizable calendar app than Apple Calendar, then Fantastical is definitely worth considering. It syncs seamlessly with Apple Calendar, so you don’t have to worry about missing any events, and it offers some great additional features and functionality. While it’s not free, many users find that the cost is worth it for the benefits it provides.

So, does Fantastical sync with Apple Calendar 2024? The answer is a resounding yes.
