Introduction

As we approach the end of the year 2023, many students and faculty members of Calvin University are looking forward to the upcoming academic year 2024-25. What is Calvin University?

Calvin University is a private Christian liberal arts college located in Grand Rapids, Michigan. It was founded in 1876 and is named after the famous theologian John Calvin. The university offers over 100 majors and programs, including pre-professional programs in fields such as medicine, law, and engineering.

What is the Academic Calendar?

The academic calendar is a schedule of events and important dates for the upcoming academic year. It includes information such as the start and end dates of the academic year, holidays, breaks, and important deadlines for registration, add/drop, and tuition payments.

New Additions to the Academic Calendar

There are some exciting new additions to the academic calendar for the year 2024-25. One of the most notable changes is the addition of a fall break. This break will take place from October 11-15, 2024 and will provide students with a much-needed break during the busy fall semester.

Another addition to the academic calendar is the inclusion of a reading day. This day will take place on May 2, 2025, and will give students a day to prepare for their final exams.

FAQs

What is the significance of the academic calendar?

The academic calendar is significant because it provides students and faculty members with a schedule of events and important dates for the upcoming academic year. It helps to ensure that everyone is on the same page and aware of important deadlines and events.

What is the purpose of the fall break?

The fall break is designed to give students a break during the busy fall semester. It provides them with an opportunity to catch up on assignments, rest, and recharge before heading into the second half of the semester.

Why was the reading day added?

The reading day was added to provide students with a day to prepare for their final exams. It gives them an opportunity to review their notes, ask questions, and get organized before the stress of finals week begins.
