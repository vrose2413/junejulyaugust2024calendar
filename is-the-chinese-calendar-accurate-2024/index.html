What is the Chinese Calendar?

The Chinese calendar is a complex system that is based on astronomical observations. The calendar is made up of a 12-year cycle, with each year named after an animal. The animal that corresponds to a particular year is said to have a significant influence on the individual born in that year.

How is the Chinese Calendar Calculated?

The Chinese calendar is based on a combination of lunar and solar cycles. The lunar cycle is around 29.5 days long, and the solar cycle is around 365.25 days long. To reconcile these two cycles, a leap month is added to the calendar every few years.

The accuracy of the Chinese calendar has been a topic of debate for many years. Many people believe that the calendar is accurate, while others are skeptical. The truth is that the Chinese calendar is not 100% accurate, as it is based on astronomical observations that are subject to change.
