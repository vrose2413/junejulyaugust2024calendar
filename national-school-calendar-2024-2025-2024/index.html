What Is the National School Calendar?

The national school calendar is a framework that guides the scheduling of academic activities in all public and private schools in the country. It outlines the start and end dates of each term, including the mid-term breaks and holidays. The calendar is developed by the Ministry of Education and is usually released a few months before the start of the academic year.

What Are the Key Dates in the 2024-2025 Academic Year?

The 2024-2025 academic year will run from September 2, 2024, to August 2, 2025. There will be three terms, with the first term starting on September 2, 2024, and ending on December 20, 2024. The second term will run from January 6, 2025, to April 3, 2025, while the third term will start on April 23, 2025, and end on August 2, 2025.

First Term

The first term will last for 16 weeks, with a mid-term break from October 23, 2024, to October 27, 2024. The term will end with a two-week holiday from December 21, 2024, to January 5, 2025.

Second Term

The second term will also last for 16 weeks, with a mid-term break from February 26, 2025, to March 2, 2025. There will be a two-week holiday from April 4, 2025, to April 20, 2025, to mark the Easter celebrations.

Third Term

The third term will run for 15 weeks, with no mid-term break. The term will end with a two-week holiday from August 3, 2025, to August 17, 2025.

What Changes Can We Expect in the 2024-2025 Academic Year?

The 2024-2025 academic year will see a few changes in the national school calendar. One of the most notable changes is the revised exam schedule. The Kenya Certificate of Primary Education (KCPE) exams will be held from October 29, 2024, to November 1, 2024, while the Kenya Certificate of Secondary Education (KCSE) exams will be held from November 4, 2024, to November 27, 2024. This change is aimed at giving students more time to prepare for the exams.

What Should Students and Parents Do to Prepare for the Academic Year?

As we approach the 2024-2025 academic year, it's crucial for students and parents to start preparing early. Here are a few tips to help you get started:

1. Mark the Key Dates on Your Calendar

Make sure you have the key dates for the academic year marked on your calendar. This will help you plan your schedule accordingly and avoid any last-minute rush.

2. Plan Your Holidays

Take advantage of the holiday periods to plan your family vacations or catch up on some rest. Make sure you plan your holidays well in advance to avoid any surprises.

3. Get the Necessary School Supplies

Make a list of all the school supplies you'll need for the academic year, including textbooks, stationery, and uniforms. This will help you budget for the expenses and avoid any last-minute rush.
