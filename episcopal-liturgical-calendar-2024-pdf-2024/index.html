What is the Liturgical Calendar?

The liturgical calendar is a system of seasons, feasts, and commemorations that guide the worship and prayer of the Church. It is based on the life of Jesus Christ and the history of the Church, and it provides a framework for the celebration of the Eucharist and other sacraments. The liturgical year begins with Advent, which prepares us for the coming of Christ, and ends with Christ the King Sunday, which celebrates Christ's reign over all creation.

What are the Seasons of the Liturgical Year?

The liturgical year is divided into several seasons, each with its own focus and character. The seasons are:

  • Advent
  • Christmas
  • Epiphany
  • Lent
  • Easter
  • Pentecost
  • Ordinary Time

Each season has a different color associated with it, which is reflected in the vestments and decorations used in worship. Advent and Lent are times of penitence and preparation, while Christmas and Easter are times of joy and celebration. Ordinary Time is the longest season and is a time for growth and reflection.

What are the Feasts and Commemorations?

The liturgical calendar also includes feasts and commemorations of important events and figures in the history of the Church. Some of the major feasts and commemorations in the Episcopal liturgical calendar for 2024 include:

  • The Feast of the Epiphany
  • Ash Wednesday

  • The Feast of the Annunciation
  • Good Friday

  • The Feast of the Ascension
  • Pentecost Sunday
  • The Feast of All Saints
  • Christ the King Sunday

Each feast and commemoration has its own readings and prayers, which reflect the theme and significance of the event or person being celebrated.
