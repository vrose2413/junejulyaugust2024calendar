What Is the Hebrew Calendar?

The Hebrew calendar is a lunisolar calendar that is used in Jewish communities around the world. It is based on the cycles of the moon and the sun, and is used to determine the dates of Jewish holidays and other important events.

How Is the Hebrew Calendar Calculated?

The Hebrew calendar is calculated based on a 19-year cycle that consists of 12 normal years and 7 leap years. In a leap year, an extra month is added to the calendar to ensure that the lunar and solar cycles stay in sync. The months of the Hebrew calendar are based on the phases of the moon, with each month beginning on the new moon.

What Is Shevat?

Shevat is the fifth month of the Hebrew calendar, and usually falls in January or February on the Gregorian calendar. It is a time of new growth and renewal, and is associated with the almond tree.

What Happens in Shevat?

Shevat is the month when the trees begin to bud and the first signs of spring appear. It is also the month when the Jewish holiday of Tu B'Shevat is celebrated. This holiday is sometimes called the "New Year of the Trees" and is a time for planting and celebrating the natural world.
