What is an Auspicious Day in Hindu Calendar?

In Hinduism, an auspicious day is a day that is believed to bring good fortune, success, and prosperity. These days are determined by astrological calculations and are often associated with religious ceremonies, festivals, and rituals. Hindus believe that performing these ceremonies on auspicious days can bring them closer to their gods and goddesses and bring them good luck.

How is the Hindu Calendar Calculated?

The Hindu calendar is a lunar calendar that is based on the cycles of the moon. It is divided into 12 months, and each month is named after a specific constellation. The Hindu calendar also takes into account the movements of the sun and the planets, as well as other astrological factors. This makes the Hindu calendar one of the most intricate and complex calendars in the world.
