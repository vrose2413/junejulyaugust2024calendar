About Texas Tech University

Texas Tech University, also known as Texas Tech or TTU, is a public research university in Lubbock, Texas. It was founded in 1923 and has since then grown into one of the largest universities in the state. The university offers over 150 undergraduate, graduate, and doctoral degree programs across 13 colleges and schools.

What is an Academic Calendar?

An academic calendar is a schedule of important dates and deadlines for the academic year. It includes the start and end dates for each semester, as well as holidays, exams, and other important events. The academic calendar helps students and faculty stay organized and plan their schedules accordingly.

The Academic Calendar for 2024

The academic calendar for Texas Tech University for the year 2024 has been released. The dates and deadlines for the fall, spring, and summer semesters are as follows:

Fall Semester

The fall semester will begin on August 26, 2024, and end on December 13, 2024. There will be a Labor Day holiday on September 2, 2024, and a Thanksgiving holiday from November 28-29, 2024. Final exams will take place from December 9-13, 2024.

Spring Semester

The spring semester will begin on January 13, 2025, and end on May 2, 2025. There will be a Martin Luther King Jr. holiday on January 20, 2025, and a Spring Break from March 9-13, 2025. Final exams will take place from April 28-May 2, 2025.

Summer Semester

The summer semester will begin on May 20, 2025, and end on August 8, 2025. There will be Memorial Day and Independence Day holidays on May 26, 2025, and July 4, 2025, respectively. Final exams will take place from August 4-8, 2025.

FAQs

When should I register for classes?

Registration for classes typically opens a few months before the start of each semester. Students should check the academic calendar for specific dates and deadlines. It is recommended that students register as early as possible to ensure they get the classes they need and avoid any late fees.

What if I need to drop a class?

Students may drop a class without penalty within the first few weeks of the semester. The specific deadline for dropping a class can be found on the academic calendar. After the deadline, students may still drop a class, but they may receive a W or WF on their transcript, which could affect their GPA.

What if I need to take a leave of absence?

If a student needs to take a leave of absence for personal or medical reasons, they should speak with their academic advisor. The advisor can help the student navigate the process and ensure they are able to return to school when they are ready.

Can I take classes online?

Texas Tech University offers a variety of online courses and degree programs. Students should check with their academic advisor to see if online classes are available for their major or degree program.

What if I have more questions?

Students should always check with their academic advisor or the university registrar for any questions or concerns about the academic calendar or other university policies. The university also has a website with additional resources and contact information.
