What is a Jewish Calendar?

A Jewish calendar is a calendar that is based on the Jewish lunar calendar, which is used to determine the dates of Jewish holidays and events. It's different from the Gregorian calendar that is used in most of the world, which is based on the solar calendar.

The Jewish calendar is used to determine the dates of important holidays like Rosh Hashanah, Yom Kippur, and Passover, as well as the weekly Sabbath. It's an important part of Jewish tradition and helps to keep the community connected and informed.
