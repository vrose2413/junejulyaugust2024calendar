What is the 4-5-4 Calendar?

The 4-5-4 calendar divides the year into quarters, with each quarter consisting of 13 weeks. The first and last quarters have 4 weeks and the second and third quarters have 5 weeks. This calendar is used by retailers to align their financial reporting periods with the natural flow of their business. The 4-5-4 calendar is also used to plan for sales and promotions, as well as to manage inventory levels.

Why Use the 4-5-4 Calendar?

The 4-5-4 calendar is preferred by retailers because it provides a consistent and predictable schedule for financial reporting. This allows retailers to more accurately analyze their business performance and plan for the future. The 4-5-4 calendar also provides a framework for planning sales and promotions, as well as managing inventory levels. By aligning their business activities with this calendar, retailers can better meet the demands of their customers.

Creating a 4-5-4 Calendar in Excel 2024

Excel 2024 is a powerful tool for creating a 4-5-4 calendar. To create a calendar in Excel, start by opening a new workbook and creating a new sheet. In the first row of the sheet, enter the names of the four quarters of the year. In the second row, enter the dates for each week of the year. You can then use conditional formatting to highlight the weeks that fall within each quarter.

Step 1: Open a New Workbook

To create a 4-5-4 calendar in Excel 2024, start by opening a new workbook. You can do this by clicking on the File menu and selecting New Workbook.

Step 2: Create a New Sheet

Once you have a new workbook open, create a new sheet by clicking on the plus sign at the bottom of the screen. Rename this sheet "Calendar".

Step 3: Enter the Quarters

In the first row of the "Calendar" sheet, enter the names of the four quarters of the year. These should be in cells A1, B1, C1, and D1.

Step 4: Enter the Dates

In the second row of the "Calendar" sheet, enter the dates for each week of the year. You can do this manually or you can use a formula to generate the dates automatically. To enter the dates manually, start with cell A2 and enter the date for the first week of the year. Continue entering the dates for each subsequent week until you reach the end of the year.

Step 5: Highlight the Quarters

To make it easier to see which weeks fall within each quarter, you can use conditional formatting to highlight the quarters. To do this, select cells A1 to D1 and click on the Home tab. Then, click on the Conditional Formatting button and select "New Rule". Choose "Use a formula to determine which cells to format" and enter the formula =COLUMN()<=4 for the first quarter, =AND(COLUMN()>4,COLUMN()<=9) for the second quarter, =AND(COLUMN()>9,COLUMN()<=13) for the third quarter, and =COLUMN()>13 for the fourth quarter. Choose a formatting style and click OK.
