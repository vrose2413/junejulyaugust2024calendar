Liturgical Color Calendar

The liturgical color calendar is a guide to the colors used in the church throughout the year. The colors used in the church signify different aspects of the faith and the liturgical year. The colors used are white, green, purple, red, and black.

The color white is used to symbolize purity, holiness, and joy. This color is used during the Christmas and Easter seasons. Green is used to symbolize growth, renewal, and hope and is used during the seasons of Ordinary Time. Purple is used to symbolize penance, preparation, and royalty and is used during the seasons of Advent and Lent. Red is used to symbolize the Holy Spirit, fire, and martyrdom and is used on Pentecost and other feasts of the Holy Spirit. Black is used to symbolize mourning and is used during funerals and other times of mourning.
