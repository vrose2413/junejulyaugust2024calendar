What is Torah Portion Calendar?

Torah Portion Calendar is a schedule that outlines the weekly Torah portions read in synagogues around the world. It covers the entire Torah in a yearly cycle, with each portion being read on a specific Shabbat. The cycle starts and ends on Simchat Torah, which is a holiday that celebrates the completion of the Torah reading.

What is the Torah?

The Torah is the central text of Judaism, consisting of the first five books of the Hebrew Bible. It contains the foundational stories and laws of Judaism and is considered to be the word of God as revealed to Moses on Mount Sinai.

Why is the Torah Portion Calendar important?

The Torah Portion Calendar is important because it ensures that the entire Torah is read and studied within a year. It also provides a sense of unity among the Jewish community, as Jews around the world are reading and studying the same portions each week.

What is the Torah Portion Calendar for 2024-2025?

The Torah Portion Calendar for 2024-2025 is as follows:

  • Bereshit (Genesis 1:1-6:8) - October 5, 2024
  • Noach (Genesis 6:9-11:32) - October 12, 2024
  • Lech Lecha (Genesis 12:1-17:27) - October 19, 2024
  • Vayeira (Genesis 18:1-22:24) - October 26, 2024
  • Chayei Sarah (Genesis 23:1-25:18) - November 2, 2024
  • Toldot (Genesis 25:19-28:9) - November 9, 2024
  • Vayetzei (Genesis 28:10-32:3) - November 16, 2024
  • Vayishlach (Genesis 32:4-36:43) - November 23, 2024
  • Vayeshev (Genesis 37:1-40:23) - November 30, 2024
  • Miketz (Genesis 41:1-44:17) - December 7, 2024

What are some traditions associated with Torah reading?

Traditionally, the Torah is read aloud in Hebrew during synagogue services. The reader, or ba'al kriyah, chants the portion while standing at the bimah, or reading desk. The congregation follows along in their own copies of the Torah, which are often ornately decorated and kept in a special ark in the synagogue.

What is the significance of the Torah reading?

The Torah reading is a central part of Jewish worship and provides a way for Jews to connect with their history and tradition. It also serves as a source of inspiration and guidance for daily life, as the stories and laws contained within the Torah are seen as relevant to modern times.

How can I follow the Torah Portion Calendar?

The Torah Portion Calendar is widely available online and in Jewish calendars and prayer books. Many synagogues also post the weekly portion on their websites or bulletin boards. By following the calendar, you can join with Jews around the world in studying and reflecting on the timeless wisdom of the Torah.

Conclusion

The Torah Portion Calendar is a vital part of Jewish life and provides a way for Jews to connect with their heritage and community. By following the calendar, you can deepen your knowledge and appreciation of the Torah and join with Jews around the world in a shared spiritual journey.
