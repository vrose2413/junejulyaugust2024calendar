Introduction

The Islamic or Hijri calendar is a lunar calendar consisting of 12 months in a year of 354 or 355 days. It is used by Muslims worldwide for religious purposes, such as determining the dates for Islamic holidays and events. In this article, we will discuss what year it is in the Muslim calendar in 2024.

What Is the Muslim Calendar?

The Muslim calendar, also known as the Hijri calendar, is based on the moon's phases, unlike the Gregorian calendar, which is a solar calendar. The Islamic calendar consists of 12 months, and each month starts with the sighting of the new moon. The 12 months in the Muslim calendar are:

  • Muharram
  • Safar
  • Rabi al-Awwal
  • Rabi al-Thani
  • Jumada al-Awwal
  • Jumada al-Thani
  • Rajab
  • Sha'ban
  • Ramadan
  • Shawwal
  • Dhu al-Qidah
  • Dhu al-Hijjah

What Year Is It in the Muslim Calendar in 2024?

In the Muslim calendar, the year 2024 corresponds to the Hijri year 1446. The Islamic calendar began with the migration of Prophet Muhammad (PBUH) from Mecca to Medina in 622 CE. The first month of the Muslim calendar, Muharram, corresponds to the migration year.

Why Is the Muslim Calendar Important?

The Muslim calendar is important because it determines the dates for Islamic holidays and events, such as Ramadan, Eid al-Fitr, and Eid al-Adha. It also helps Muslims keep track of important religious events and occasions, such as the Hajj pilgrimage to Mecca.

Question and Answer

Q: How do Muslims celebrate the start of a new year in the Muslim calendar?

A: Muslims do not celebrate the start of a new year in the Muslim calendar with any special events or festivities. However, some Muslims may choose to reflect on their spiritual journey and renew their commitment to their faith.

Q: Is the Muslim calendar used only by Muslims?

A: While the Muslim calendar is primarily used by Muslims for religious purposes, it is also used by some non-Muslims for cultural and commercial purposes in Muslim-majority countries.

Q: Is the Muslim calendar the same in all countries?

A: The Muslim calendar is the same in all countries, but there may be slight variations in the start of the months due to differences in the sighting of the new moon.

Conclusion

In conclusion, the Muslim calendar is an important aspect of Islam and is used by Muslims worldwide for religious purposes. In 2024, the year in the Muslim calendar is 1446. Understanding the Muslim calendar is essential for anyone interested in learning about Islamic culture and traditions.
