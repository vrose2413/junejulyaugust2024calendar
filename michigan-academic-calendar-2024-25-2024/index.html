What is the Michigan Academic Calendar?

The Michigan Academic Calendar is a schedule of important dates for the academic year in Michigan. This calendar includes dates for the first day of school, holidays, and semester breaks. The academic calendar is created by the Michigan Department of Education and is followed by all public schools and some private schools in Michigan.

When does the academic year start?

The academic year in Michigan typically starts in early September. For the year 2024-25, the first day of school is scheduled for September 3, 2024. However, some schools may have different start dates, so it is important to check with your school for their specific start date.

What are the holidays in the academic calendar?

The Michigan academic calendar includes several holidays throughout the year. These holidays include Labor Day, Thanksgiving, Christmas, Martin Luther King Jr. Day, Presidents' Day, Memorial Day, and Independence Day. The specific dates for each holiday may vary from year to year.

When are the semester breaks?

The Michigan academic calendar includes two semester breaks. The first semester break is scheduled for December 23, 2024, to January 3, 2025. The second semester break is scheduled for March 28, 2025, to April 5, 2025. These breaks are important for students and teachers to rest and recharge before returning to school.

How can I access the Michigan Academic Calendar?

The Michigan Academic Calendar is available online on the Michigan Department of Education website. You can also access the calendar through your school's website or by contacting your school directly. It is important to regularly check the calendar for any changes or updates.

What are the benefits of using the Michigan Academic Calendar?

The Michigan Academic Calendar is an essential tool for planning your academic year. By knowing the important dates in advance, you can plan your schedule accordingly and avoid conflicts with other events. The academic calendar also helps teachers and parents plan their schedules and ensure that students are on track to meet their academic goals.
