What is the Second Circuit Court of Appeals?

The Second Circuit Court of Appeals is one of the thirteen courts of appeals in the United States. It is located in New York City and has jurisdiction over the states of Connecticut, New York, and Vermont. The court handles appeals from federal district courts and federal agencies.

What is an oral argument?

An oral argument is a presentation made to the judges of a court by an attorney on behalf of their client. The attorney presents their case and responds to questions from the judges. Oral arguments are an important part of the legal process because they allow judges to ask questions and clarify issues before making a decision.

Why is the Second Circuit oral argument calendar important?

The Second Circuit oral argument calendar is important because it provides an opportunity for lawyers and law students to observe the legal process in action. Attending an oral argument can help them understand how the court works and how judges make decisions. It also allows them to see how attorneys present their arguments and respond to questions from the judges.

How can I attend the Second Circuit oral argument calendar?

The Second Circuit oral argument calendar is open to the public. You can attend by visiting the Thurgood Marshall Courthouse in New York City or by watching the live stream on the court's website. The court also provides audio recordings of the oral arguments.
