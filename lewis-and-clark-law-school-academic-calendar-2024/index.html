When Does the Academic Year Start?

The academic year at Lewis and Clark Law School for 2024 starts on August 26, 2024. This is when classes begin for the fall semester. It's important to note that this date is subject to change, so it's always a good idea to double-check with the school's website closer to the start of the semester.

What Are the Important Dates to Remember?

There are several important dates to remember for the 2024 academic year at Lewis and Clark Law School. Here are some of the most significant:

  • August 26, 2024: First day of fall semester classes
  • September 2, 2024: Labor Day (no classes)
  • November 28-29, 2024: Thanksgiving break (no classes)
  • December 9-18, 2024: Fall semester exams
  • January 13, 2025: First day of spring semester classes

What Should You Expect?

Law school is notoriously rigorous, and the academic calendar reflects that. Students at Lewis and Clark Law School can expect a full course load, with classes meeting multiple times per week. There will also be reading assignments, papers, and exams throughout the semester. It's important to stay organized and manage your time effectively to succeed in law school.

What Resources Are Available to Help You?

Fortunately, Lewis and Clark Law School offers a variety of resources to help students succeed. The law library is an excellent resource for research, and there are also academic support programs and writing centers available. Additionally, professors and advisors are always willing to provide guidance and support to students.

Question and Answer

Q: How many classes should I take per semester?

A: At Lewis and Clark Law School, students typically take between 12-16 credit hours per semester. This is equivalent to 3-4 classes. It's important to balance your course load with other commitments, such as work or extracurricular activities.

Q: What happens if I need to miss a class?

A: It's important to attend all classes, but if you do need to miss one, it's a good idea to let your professor know in advance. You should also make arrangements to catch up on any missed material or assignments.

Q: Is there a dress code at Lewis and Clark Law School?

A: There is no official dress code at Lewis and Clark Law School, but students are expected to dress professionally. This means no jeans or flip-flops, and clothing should be neat and clean. Additionally, some classes may require business attire for presentations or mock trials.
