Introduction

The Las Cruces Public School Calendar for 2024-2025 is now available. This calendar provides a schedule of all the important dates for the upcoming academic year. It is a great tool for parents, students, and teachers to plan their schedules accordingly.

When does the new school year start?

The new school year for Las Cruces Public Schools will begin on August 19, 2024. This is a Monday, and it will be a full day of school for all students.

As the new school year approaches, now is the time to start planning for it. Parents should start making arrangements for transportation, school supplies, and after-school care if necessary. Students should start getting back into the routine of going to bed early and waking up early to prepare for the start of the school year.

What are the important dates to remember?

There are several important dates to remember for the 2024-2025 school year. Some of the notable dates include:
  • Thanksgiving Break: November 25-29, 2024
  • Winter Break: December 23, 2024 - January 3, 2025
  • Spring Break: March 16-20, 2025
  • Last Day of School: May 23, 2025

Are there any changes to the calendar?

There are no major changes to the Las Cruces Public School Calendar for 2024-2025. However, it is important to note that the calendar is subject to change in the event of inclement weather or other unforeseen circumstances.

Parents and students should regularly check the Las Cruces Public Schools website or social media pages for any updates or changes to the calendar.

Tips for a Successful School Year

Get Organized

One of the best ways to ensure a successful school year is to get organized. This means having a designated study area at home, keeping track of assignments and deadlines, and creating a schedule for homework and studying.

Stay Involved

Another important tip for a successful school year is to stay involved. This means attending parent-teacher conferences, volunteering at school events, and joining extracurricular activities.

Communicate with Teachers

It is important to communicate with your child's teachers throughout the school year. This means attending parent-teacher conferences, reaching out with any concerns or questions, and staying up-to-date on your child's progress.
