How to Transfer Meeting Ownership in Google Calendar 2024

Introduction

Google Calendar is a popular tool used for scheduling and organizing events. One of its features is the ability to assign ownership of meetings to other users. This can be useful when the original organizer is unable to attend the meeting, or when they are no longer responsible for the event. In this article, we will discuss how to transfer meeting ownership in Google Calendar 2024.

Step 1: Open Google Calendar

The first step is to open Google Calendar in your web browser. You can access it by going to https://calendar.google.com/ and logging in with your Google account.

Step 2: Select the Meeting

Next, select the meeting that you want to transfer ownership of. You can do this by clicking on the event in your calendar.

Step 3: Click on the Three Dots

Once you have selected the meeting, click on the three dots in the top right corner of the event details.

Step 4: Click on "Change Owner"

From the dropdown menu, click on "Change Owner". This will bring up a list of users who have access to the event.

Step 5: Select the New Owner

Select the user that you want to transfer ownership to from the list. You can also search for users by typing their name in the search bar.

Step 6: Click "Save"

Once you have selected the new owner, click on "Save" to transfer ownership of the meeting. The new owner will receive an email notification informing them of the change.

Benefits of Transferring Meeting Ownership

Transferring ownership of meetings in Google Calendar can be beneficial in a number of ways. Some of the benefits include:

Improved Collaboration

By transferring ownership, you can ensure that the right person is responsible for the meeting. This can help to improve collaboration and ensure that the meeting is productive.

Increased Flexibility

Transferring ownership can also provide increased flexibility. If the original organizer is unable to attend the meeting, someone else can step in and take over.

Reduced Workload

Finally, transferring ownership can help to reduce the workload of the original organizer. If they are no longer responsible for the event, they can focus on other tasks.

Q&A

Q: Can I transfer ownership of a recurring meeting?

A: Yes, you can transfer ownership of a recurring meeting. When you transfer ownership, all future instances of the meeting will also be transferred to the new owner.

Q: Can I transfer ownership of a meeting to someone outside of my organization?

A: No, you can only transfer ownership of a meeting to someone who has access to your organization's Google Calendar. If you want to transfer ownership to someone outside of your organization, you will need to share the event with them and give them permission to make changes.

Q: What happens to the original organizer's calendar when they transfer ownership of a meeting?

A: When ownership of a meeting is transferred, the event will be removed from the original organizer's calendar. However, they will still receive notifications about the event unless they choose to unsubscribe.
