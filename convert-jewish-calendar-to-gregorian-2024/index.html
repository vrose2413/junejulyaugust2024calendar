What is the Jewish Calendar?

The Jewish calendar is a lunar calendar that starts with the creation of the world, according to Jewish tradition. It has 12 months, with an extra month added in some years to align with the solar cycle. The months are named after Babylonian gods and have different lengths ranging from 29 to 30 days.

What is the Gregorian Calendar?

The Gregorian calendar is a solar calendar that was introduced by Pope Gregory XIII in 1582. It is the most widely used calendar in the world and has 12 months with 365 days in a regular year and 366 days in a leap year.
