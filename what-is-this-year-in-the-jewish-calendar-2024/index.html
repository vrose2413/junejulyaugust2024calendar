How does the Jewish calendar work?

The Jewish calendar is based on the cycles of the moon and the sun. Each month begins with the sighting of the new moon, and the length of each month is determined by the time it takes for the moon to complete one orbit around the Earth. However, since the lunar year is about 11 days shorter than the solar year, the Jewish calendar adds a leap month every few years to make up for the discrepancy.
