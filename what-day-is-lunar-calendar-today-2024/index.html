What is the Lunar Calendar?

The lunar calendar is a calendar based on the cycles of the moon. It is different from the Gregorian calendar, which is based on the cycles of the sun. The lunar calendar is widely used in many parts of the world, especially in Asia, to determine important dates and events such as festivals, holidays, and religious observances.

How Does the Lunar Calendar Work?

The lunar calendar is based on the cycles of the moon, which takes approximately 29.5 days to complete one cycle. The lunar calendar consists of 12 months, each with 29 or 30 days. In order to synchronize the lunar calendar with the solar calendar, a leap month is added every few years. This ensures that important dates such as the Chinese New Year and the Islamic holy month of Ramadan fall on the same season every year.
