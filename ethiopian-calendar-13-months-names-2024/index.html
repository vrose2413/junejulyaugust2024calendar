Introduction

Ethiopia is known for having a unique calendar system that is different from the Gregorian calendar used by most of the world. The Ethiopian calendar has 13 months instead of 12, and it is based on the ancient Coptic calendar.

What is the Ethiopian Calendar?

The Ethiopian calendar has 13 months, with 12 months having 30 days each, and the 13th month having 5 or 6 days depending on whether it is a leap year or not. The Ethiopian calendar is also about 7 years and 8 months behind the Gregorian calendar.

What are the Months of the Ethiopian Calendar?

The months of the Ethiopian calendar are:

  • Meskerem
  • Tikimt
  • Hidar
  • Tahsas
  • Tir
  • Yekatit
  • Megabit
  • Miazia
  • Genbot
  • Sene
  • Hamle
  • Nehase
  • Pagume

The 13th month, Pagume, is added to the calendar to make up for the difference between the Ethiopian and Gregorian calendars.

What is the Significance of the Ethiopian Calendar?

The Ethiopian calendar is significant in Ethiopian culture and religion. It is used to determine the dates of religious holidays and festivals, such as Timkat and Meskel. It is also used in agriculture to determine the planting and harvesting seasons.
