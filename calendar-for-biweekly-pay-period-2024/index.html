What is a Biweekly Pay Period?

A biweekly pay period is a payment schedule in which employees are paid every two weeks. This means that there are 26 pay periods in a year. Biweekly pay periods are common in many industries, including retail, healthcare, and food service.

How Does the Biweekly Pay Period Calendar Work?

The biweekly pay period calendar is a schedule that outlines the pay periods for the year. In 2024, the first biweekly pay period will begin on January 1st and end on January 14th. The last biweekly pay period will begin on December 16th and end on December 31st.

Why is the Biweekly Pay Period Calendar Important?

The biweekly pay period calendar is important because it ensures that employees receive their paychecks on time. It also helps employers stay organized and avoid any mistakes or delays in payment. By understanding the biweekly pay period calendar, both employers and employees can plan their finances accordingly.
