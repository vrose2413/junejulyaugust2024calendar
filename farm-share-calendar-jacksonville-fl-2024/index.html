Introduction: What is a Farm Share Calendar?

A farm share calendar is a schedule of when different types of produce are available from local farms. It is a helpful tool for people who want to eat fresh, locally-grown fruits and vegetables.

Why Use a Farm Share Calendar?

Using a farm share calendar allows you to plan your meals around what is in season. This can help you save money on groceries and support local farmers. It also ensures that you are eating fresh, nutritious food that is good for your health.

The Benefits of Eating Locally-Grown Produce

There are many benefits to eating locally-grown produce. Here are just a few:

1. It is Fresh and Nutritious

When you buy produce from a local farm, it is usually picked within a day or two of being sold. This means that it is fresher and more nutritious than produce that has been shipped from far away.

2. It Supports Local Farmers

Buying locally-grown produce supports the local economy and helps small farmers stay in business. This is important because many small farmers struggle to compete with large, industrial farms.

3. It is Better for the Environment

Buying locally-grown produce reduces the carbon footprint of your food. This is because it does not have to be transported long distances, which requires a lot of energy and contributes to air pollution.

The Farm Share Calendar for Jacksonville FL in 2024

Here is the farm share calendar for Jacksonville FL in 2024:

January-March

During these months, you can expect to find citrus fruits, such as oranges and grapefruits, as well as leafy greens like kale and lettuce.

April-June

As the weather warms up, you can expect to find strawberries, blueberries, and other types of berries, as well as tomatoes, cucumbers, and peppers.

July-September

During the summer months, you can expect to find sweet corn, watermelon, and other types of melons, as well as peaches and other stone fruits.

October-December

As the weather cools down, you can expect to find apples, pumpkins, and other types of squash, as well as root vegetables like carrots and potatoes.

Question and Answer

Q: Where can I find a farm share calendar for my area?

A: You can usually find a farm share calendar by doing a quick internet search for your city or region. Many local farms also have calendars posted on their websites or social media pages.

Q: How do I know if a local farm is reputable?

A: It is always a good idea to do some research before buying produce from a local farm. Look for reviews online and ask around to see if anyone you know has had a positive experience with the farm. You can also look for certification from organizations like Certified Naturally Grown or USDA Organic.

Q: What should I do with produce that is in season?

A: There are many ways to use in-season produce. Try incorporating it into your favorite recipes or using it as inspiration for new dishes. You can also preserve produce by canning, freezing, or drying it for later use.
