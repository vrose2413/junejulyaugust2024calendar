Introduction

Baltimore County residents have always been conscious about keeping their environment clean and hygienic. One of the most important aspects of this is proper waste management. In this article, we will provide you with all the information you need to know about the Baltimore County Trash Pickup Calendar for the year 2024.

What is the Baltimore County Trash Pickup Calendar?

The Baltimore County Trash Pickup Calendar is a schedule that indicates the days on which the county's Department of Public Works will collect trash and other waste materials from residential areas. This calendar is updated every year to ensure that residents are aware of the collection days for their specific neighborhoods.

When will the Trash Pickup Calendar for 2024 be released?

The exact date for the release of the Baltimore County Trash Pickup Calendar for 2024 has not been announced yet. However, based on previous years, it is expected to be released in December 2023 or January 2024. Residents can check the official website of the county's Department of Public Works for updates.

Trash Pickup Schedule

How often is trash collected?

Trash is collected once a week in Baltimore County. However, the exact day of collection varies based on the location of your residence. The Trash Pickup Calendar provides specific information on collection days for each neighborhood.

What items are collected?

The Department of Public Works collects regular household trash, recycling materials, yard waste, and bulk items. However, there are specific guidelines for each type of material. For instance, recycling materials need to be separated into different containers, and yard waste needs to be placed in paper bags or reusable containers.

What are the guidelines for bulk items?

Bulk items such as furniture, mattresses, and appliances are collected separately from regular household trash. Residents need to schedule a pickup for these items by calling the Department of Public Works. There is a fee for this service.

Recycling Schedule

When is recycling collected?

Recycling is collected once every other week in Baltimore County. The exact day of collection again varies based on the location of your residence. The Recycling Pickup Calendar provides specific information on collection days for each neighborhood.

What can be recycled?

Baltimore County residents can recycle a variety of materials, including paper, cardboard, plastic bottles and containers, aluminum cans, and glass bottles and jars. However, there are specific guidelines for each type of material. For example, plastic bottles and containers need to be rinsed and placed in a separate container.

What cannot be recycled?

Some materials cannot be recycled in Baltimore County, including plastic bags, Styrofoam, and shredded paper. These items need to be disposed of in regular household trash.

Conclusion

Keeping our environment clean and healthy is everyone's responsibility. Proper waste management is an integral part of this. By following the Baltimore County Trash Pickup Calendar for 2024, residents can ensure that their waste is collected efficiently and in a timely manner. If you have any further questions or concerns, please contact the Department of Public Works.

Question and Answer

Q: Can I dispose of hazardous waste in regular household trash?

No. Hazardous waste such as chemicals, batteries, and electronics cannot be disposed of in regular household trash. Residents need to take these items to designated drop-off locations or schedule a pickup through the Department of Public Works.

Q: What should I do if my trash or recycling is missed on collection day?

If your trash or recycling is missed on collection day, you can report it to the Department of Public Works by calling 410-887-2000. They will schedule a pickup for the missed items as soon as possible.

Q: Can I dispose of yard waste in regular household trash?

No. Yard waste such as leaves, grass, and branches cannot be disposed of in regular household trash. Residents need to place these items in paper bags or reusable containers and place them at the curb on designated collection days.
