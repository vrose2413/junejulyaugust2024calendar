What is Dekalb County Sanitation?

Dekalb County Sanitation is responsible for collecting and disposing of garbage, recycling, and yard waste for residents of Dekalb County. The department also provides litter control and street sweeping services. The sanitation department operates six days a week, Monday through Saturday, and is closed on Sundays and major holidays.

What are the Holidays in 2024?

In 2024, Dekalb County Sanitation will observe the following holidays:

  • New Year's Day - January 1, 2024
  • Martin Luther King Jr. Day - January 15, 2024
  • Memorial Day - May 27, 2024
  • Independence Day - July 4, 2024
  • Labor Day - September 2, 2024
  • Thanksgiving Day - November 28, 2024
  • Christmas Day - December 25, 2024

How Will the Holidays Affect Sanitation Services?

During the holidays, Dekalb County Sanitation will operate on a modified schedule. Garbage, recycling, and yard waste will not be collected on the observed holiday. Instead, collection will be delayed by one day for the rest of the week. For example, if the holiday falls on a Monday, Monday's collection will occur on Tuesday, Tuesday's collection will occur on Wednesday, and so on. Friday's collection will occur on Saturday.

What Should I Do with My Trash and Recycling?

If your regular collection day falls on a holiday, you should leave your trash and recycling at the curb and it will be collected on the next scheduled collection day. Do not overload your bins or place trash or recycling on the ground. This can create a safety hazard and attract animals.

What About Bulky Items and Special Collections?

Bulky items, such as furniture and appliances, will be collected on the same schedule as regular trash. Special collections, such as electronics recycling and hazardous waste disposal, may be affected by the holiday schedule. Check the Dekalb County Sanitation website for more information on special collections.

Question and Answer

Q: What should I do if my trash or recycling is not collected on the scheduled day?

A: If your trash or recycling is not collected on the scheduled day, you should contact the sanitation department to report the missed collection. They will make arrangements to collect your trash or recycling as soon as possible.

Q: Can I put my Christmas tree out for collection?

A: Yes, Christmas trees will be collected as part of the regular yard waste collection. Trees should be cut into sections no longer than four feet and placed at the curb on your regular collection day.
