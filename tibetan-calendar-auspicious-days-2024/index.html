The Tibetan calendar is a lunisolar calendar that is based on the phases of the moon and the position of the sun. It is used by Tibetans to determine auspicious days for various activities. The calendar is divided into twelve lunar months, each of which has twenty-nine or thirty days. The Tibetan New Year falls in February or March and is celebrated for fifteen days.
