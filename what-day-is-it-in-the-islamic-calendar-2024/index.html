The Islamic Calendar

The Islamic calendar is based on the lunar cycle, which means that the months are determined by the sighting of the moon. Each month begins with the sighting of the new moon. The Islamic calendar is different from the Gregorian calendar, which is a solar calendar used in most parts of the world.

The Islamic calendar consists of 12 months, with each month having either 29 or 30 days. The months are named as follows:
